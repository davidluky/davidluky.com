MMX Phase 7 — Central Highway (Opening Stage)

Third stage validated. Same pipeline as Launch Octopus and Chill Penguin. Now with multi-frame vote-based matching: every in-state frame extracts a composite, top-1 canonical hits are bucketed by sheet+position, and the cluster with the most frames voting wins. A single-frame false positive can't outvote a consistent canonical identity.

Zero-dependency capture. highway_from_boot.lua boots the ROM fresh, taps START for 30 s to clear the title and intro, then walks X right for 60 s while interleaving Y on a 45-frame cadence to trigger hurt / death transitions. 360 screenshots at the OAM dump cadence. Display uses raw OAM-bbox crops so rim pixels sharing stage BG colors aren't trimmed off (per user feedback on the first pass); the matcher still runs on CGRAM-refined silhouettes for accuracy.
Vote-confidence bars. The bar under each state shows k / N — how many candidate frames agreed on the winning canonical position. Runners-up are listed so suspicious matches are inspectable. Left pane is the full OAM-bbox crop (what the enemy renders in that state, rim included); right pane is the matched canonical region expanded outward until every border is pure BG (the sprite's natural "imaginary black box").

Takeaways for the pipeline

  1. Voting beats single-frame top-1 in principle but inherits systematic extraction errors. With 14 state-4 frames voting, 5 of them landed on the same wrong canonical region because the per-frame extractor consistently included contamination (probably neighbor sprites sharing palette row 5). Voting rejected single-frame coincidences but can't tell the difference between "5 correct frames" and "5 wrong frames agreeing on the same mistake."
  2. Next layer: tile-ID cross-check. Each state carries a list of OAM tile IDs. The canonical sheet is itself derived from the ROM's VRAM. Decoding those tiles and pixel- comparing to the reference crop at (match_x, match_y) promotes matches from "silhouette agreement" to "content agreement." A wrong canonical region will disagree on pixel content even when the silhouette matches. Queued for next session.
  3. 180 screenshots / 1800 frames gives enough candidates for voting to converge. The earlier 30-screenshot capture (every 60 frames) left state-4 with only 2 candidates, making voting near-random. Dense screenshots + OAM-cadence sampling is now the default.
