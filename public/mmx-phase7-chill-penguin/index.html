Pipeline. For every recon frame, parse the SNES OAM table (128 entries, palette row 0..7 per entry). Skip pal 2 (HUD) and the player-X palette (positional filter: skip any cluster whose bbox sits at x≤40, y≤110). For each remaining palette row, union the on-screen OAM bboxes into a mask, intersect with the pal-N CGRAM color set, and cross-correlate the resulting silhouette against all four canonical enemy sheets from The Spriters Resource. Verdicts by Jaccard: PERFECT ≥ 0.99, STRONG ≥ 0.90, WEAK 0.70–0.90.

Takeaways for the pipeline

  1. CP runs the LO pipeline unchanged. Same extract_enemy_from_oam.py + sprite_reference_matcher.py scripts, different stage dir, six candidates surfaced with four STRONG verdicts. That's the generalization signal we needed.
  2. No PERFECT hits on CP. LO's frog scored 1.000 because its palette uses only solid fills (no half-tone outlines shared with BG). CP enemies use darker outline colors that the stage BG tiles also use, so CGRAM-refinement drops a thin rim of pixels. A future enhancement: union CGRAM colors across the first few frames of the enemy's lifetime (palette animation smooths the silhouette out).
  3. Pal 6 shows up here, not in LO. CP uses more OBJ palette rows than LO did. The pipeline auto-discovered this by sweeping all rows; no hardcoding needed.
  4. X-filter is portable. The --reject-player-slots heuristic (skip any cluster whose bbox sits at x≤40, y≤110) worked on both stages without tuning.
